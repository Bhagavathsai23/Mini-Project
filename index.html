Learn to Pay Tax Efficiently

Why Pay Tax?

Taxes are crucial for funding public services such as healthcare, education, and infrastructure. They help reduce economic inequality and support government functions.

Advantages of Paying Tax

  • Loan approvals
  • Visa applications
  • Proof of income for self-employed individuals
  • Government tenders
  • Carrying forward losses
  • Claiming tax refunds
  • High-cover life insurance
  • Compensation claims

What Are the Details You Need When E-Filing Your Income Tax Returns?

  • Basic information: PAN, Aadhar Card details, and current address.
  • Bank account details: All accounts held during the financial year.
  • Income proofs: Current salary details, income from investments (e.g., FDs, savings account).
  • Deductions claimed: Under Section 80 or Chapter VI-A.
  • Tax payment details: TDS and advance tax payments.

Tax Laws & Slabs

Old Regime

  1. Various deductions under sections like 80C, 80D, etc.
  2. Slabs vary based on age group: below 60, 60-80, over 80.

Tax Slabs

Income Range (Rs.) Tax Rate
Up to 2,50,000 Nil
2,50,001 - 5,00,000 5%
5,00,001 - 7,50,000 10%
7,50,001 - 10,00,000 15%
10,00,001 - 12,50,000 20%
12,50,001 - 15,00,000 20% + 10% surcharge
Above 15,00,000 30% + 10% surcharge

New Regime

  1. No deductions except standard deduction and certain specified deductions.
  2. Same slab for all age groups.

How to File for a Tax Refund

  • Declare Investments in Form 16: Include life insurance premiums, house rent, investments in equity/NSC/mutual funds, bank FDs, and tuition fees with necessary proofs.
  • Use Form 30: Fill out Form 30 to request a refund if you paid excess taxes.
  • Deadline: Submit your claim before the end of the financial year.
  • Accompanying Documents: Attach a return in the prescribed form (under section 139).
