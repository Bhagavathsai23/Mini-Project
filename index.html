Learn To Pay Tax

Why Pay Tax?

Taxes are essential for funding public services like healthcare, education, infrastructure, and more.

They help reduce economic inequality and support the functioning of the government.

Advantages of paying Tax

  • Loan approvals
  • Visa applications
  • Proof of income for self-employed
  • Government tenders
  • Carrying forward losses
  • Claiming tax refunds
  • High-cover life insurance
  • Compensation claims

What Are the Details You Need When You're E-Filing Your Income Tax Returns?

1. Basic information such as PAN, Aadhar Card details, and current address.

2. All the bank account details held in a financial year.

3. Income proofs like current salary details, income from investments (like FDs, savings bank account) etc.

4. All the deductions claimed under Section 80 or Chapter VI-A.

5. Tax payment details such as TDS and advance tax payments.

Tax Laws & Slabs

Old Regime

  1. Various deductions under sections like 80C, 80D, etc.
  2. Slabs vary based on age group: below 60, 60-80, over 80.

Tax Slabs

Income Range (Rs.) Tax Rate
Up to 2,50,000 Nil
2,50,001 - 5,00,000 5%
5,00,001 - 7,50,000 10%
7,50,001 - 10,00,000 15%
10,00,001 - 12,50,000 20%
12,50,001 - 15,00,000 20% + 10% surcharge
Above 15,00,000 30% + 10% surcharge

New Regime

  1. No deductions except standard deduction and certain specified deductions.
  2. Same slab for all age groups.

How to File for a Tax Refund

  • Declare Investments in Form 16: Include details like life insurance premiums, house rent, investments in equity/NSC/mutual funds, bank FDs, and tuition fees with the necessary proofs.
  • Use Form 30: If you paid excess taxes, fill out Form 30 to request a refund.
  • Deadline: Submit your claim before the end of the financial year.
  • Accompanying Documents: Attach a return in the prescribed form (under section 139).
