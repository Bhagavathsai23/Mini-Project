Tax Brackets and Rates

For the current tax year, income is taxed at progressive rates. Here are the brackets:
- 10% on income up to $9,950
- 12% on income over $9,950
- 22% on income over $40,525
- 24% on income over $86,375
- 32% on income over $164,925
- 35% on income over $209,425
- 37% on income over $523,600

Deductions and Credits

Key deductions include mortgage interest, charitable donations, and medical expenses. Important credits to explore are the Child Tax Credit and the Earned Income Tax Credit.

Filing Deadlines

Tax returns are typically due by April 15th. Extensions are available until October 15th, and estimated tax payments are due quarterly.

Tax Changes

Stay updated with recent tax law changes such as modifications in standard deduction amounts or any new tax credits introduced by recent legislation.

Filing Methods

You can file taxes electronically (e-file) for speed and accuracy, or use traditional paper filing. E-filing is generally faster and allows direct deposit.

Penalties

Be aware of penalties for late filing and underpayment. Late filing penalties can be 5% of the unpaid taxes for each month your return is late.
